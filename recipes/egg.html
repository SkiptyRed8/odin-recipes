Menudo Recipe

Filipino Menudo** – a hearty pork and liver stew in a rich tomato sauce

Filipino Menudo is a rich and savory stew made with pork, liver, and vegetables, simmered in a flavorful tomato-based sauce.

This classic dish is a comforting favorite, often served with steamed rice and enjoyed at family gatherings.

Ingridients

  • 500g pork (shoulder or belly), diced
  • 200g pork liver, diced (optional)
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste (optional, for richer flavor)
  • 1 cup water or broth
  • 1 medium potato, diced
  • 1 medium carrot, diced
  • 1 small red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small green bell pepper, diced
  • ¼ cup green peas (optional)
  • ¼ cup raisins (optional, for sweetness)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce (patis) or to taste
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional)
  • ½ tsp ground black pepper
  • Salt to taste

How to Cook

  1. Prepare the ingredients – Dice the pork, liver, potatoes, carrots, and bell peppers. Chop the onion, garlic, and tomatoes.
  2. Sauté the aromatics – Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Sauté the onion and garlic until fragrant, then add the chopped tomatoes and cook until softened.
  3. Cook the pork – Add the diced pork and sauté until lightly browned. Pour in the soy sauce and fish sauce, then cook for a few minutes to let the flavors absorb.
  4. Add the tomato sauce – Stir in the tomato sauce and tomato paste (if using). Pour in the water or broth, bring to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer for about 20 minutes or until the pork is tender.
  5. Cook the liver – Add the diced liver and cook for another 5 minutes.
  6. Add vegetables – Stir in the potatoes, carrots, and bell peppers. Simmer until the vegetables are tender, about 10 minutes.
  7. Season the dish – Add sugar (if using), ground black pepper, and salt to taste. Stir in green peas and raisins (if using), then simmer for another 2–3 minutes.
  8. Serve and enjoy – Turn off the heat and let the menudo rest for a few minutes. Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy!
