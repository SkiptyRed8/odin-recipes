Adobo Recipe

"Discover the rich and savory flavors of Adobo, a beloved Filipino dish!

This easy-to-follow recipe will guide you in making the perfect blend of tender meat, soy sauce, vinegar, and aromatic spices.

Whether you’re a beginner or a seasoned cook, this page has everything you need to create a delicious and authentic Adobo at home!"

Ingridients

  • ½ cup soy sauce
  • ¼ cup vinegar (white or cane vinegar)
  • 1 cup water (adjust as needed)
  • 1 head garlic, minced or crushed
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns (or ground black pepper)
  • 3-4 dried bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, for a slightly sweet balance)
  • salt to taste (optional)

How to Cook

  1. Prepare the Ingredients – Cut the meat into serving pieces and mince the garlic.
  2. Marinate the Meat – In a bowl, combine the meat, soy sauce, and half of the garlic. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes (or overnight for better flavor)..
  3. Sauté the Garlic – Heat oil in a pan over medium heat and sauté the remaining garlic until golden brown.
  4. Brown the Meat – Add the marinated meat to the pan (without the marinade first) and cook until lightly browned.
  5. Add the Marinade and Spices – Pour in the marinade, then add vinegar, bay leaves, black peppercorns, and water. Do not stir yet.
  6. Simmer Until Tender – Cover and let it simmer over low heat for about 30–40 minutes or until the meat is tender. Stir occasionally and add more water if needed.
  7. Adjust Flavor – Taste and add sugar or salt if desired. Let it simmer for a few more minutes to blend the flavors.
  8. Reduce the Sauce – If you prefer a thicker sauce, let it cook uncovered for a few more minutes until the sauce is slightly reduced.
  9. Serve and Enjoy – Remove from heat and serve hot with steamed rice!
